About

I am a PhD student in computational neuroscience at the Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive and Brain Sciences in Leipzig. I work in the Vision & Computational Cognition Lab (Prof. Dr. Martin Hebart) in close collaboration with Prof. Dr. Martin Rolfs.

My research focuses on the interplay between eye movements and object representations in the brain. I combine behavioural experiments, neuroimaging (fMRI & MEG), and computational modelling to understand how the visual system processes information across saccades.
